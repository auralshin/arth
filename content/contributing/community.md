#### Community Standards

**Respectful Engagement**
- Assume good intent; seek to understand before responding
- Avoid gatekeeping language ("everyone knows," "obviously," "just")
- Welcome questions at all levels of expertise
- Provide constructive feedback with actionable suggestions
- No harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks

**Technical Rigor**
- Back claims with sources, data, or reasoning
- Acknowledge uncertainty and state assumptions
- Correct errors gracefully when pointed out
- Differentiate between opinion and established fact
- Cite others' work and give credit generously

**Collaborative Spirit**
- Share knowledge freely; Arth is a public good
- Help newcomers get oriented and find resources
- Celebrate others' contributions and achievements
- Collaborate across projects and teams
- Prioritize learning and ecosystem growth

#### Ways to Contribute

**Beyond Documentation**
- **Research**: Analyze protocols, publish findings, share insights
- **Tools**: Build simulations, dashboards, or analysis scripts
- **Workshops**: Host educational sessions or AMAs
- **Translation**: Make content accessible in other languages
- **Mentorship**: Guide new contributors through their first PR
- **Curation**: Suggest improvements to existing pages

**Community Roles**
- **Contributors**: Anyone who submits PRs or feedback
- **Reviewers**: Experienced members who provide feedback on submissions
- **Maintainers**: Core team managing repository and roadmap
- **Subject Matter Experts**: Deep expertise in specific protocols or topics

Roles are earned through consistent, high-quality contributions and community engagement.

#### Collaboration Patterns

**Co-authoring Content**
- Multiple contributors can collaborate on complex topics
- Use GitHub branches and PRs for iterative development
- Credit all contributors in acknowledgments or commit history
- Coordinate on Discord or GitHub Discussions

**Peer Review Partnerships**
- Pair up with someone to review each other's work
- Exchange expertise: "I'll review your AMM math if you review my governance analysis"
- Builds relationships and improves quality

**Topic Working Groups**
- Form temporary groups for large initiatives (e.g., "Concentrated Liquidity Deep Dive")
- Coordinate via dedicated Discord channel or GitHub Project
- Regular sync meetings or async updates
- Dissolve after completion or transition to maintenance mode

**Getting Started Resources**
- Read [How to Contribute](/contributing/how-to-contribute) for workflow overview

- Browse [Roadmap](/contributing/roadmap) for suggested topics

#### Code of Conduct

**Summary**
Arth follows a professional, inclusive code of conduct. Key principles:
- Respect all participants regardless of background or experience
- Focus on ideas and substance, not personal characteristics
- No spam, self-promotion, or off-topic content in technical channels
- Protect privacy: don't share others' contact info or private discussions
- Report violations to maintainers via direct message

**Enforcement**
- Warnings for minor or first-time violations
- Temporary mute or ban for repeated or severe violations
- Permanent ban for harassment, doxxing, or illegal activity
- Maintainers have final say on moderation decisions
